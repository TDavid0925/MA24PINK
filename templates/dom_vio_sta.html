Domestic Violence Statistics

The dashboard below enlists key statistics relating to family violence incidents based on data extracted from Victoria Police Law Enforcement Assistance Program as of 18 July 2018.

The dashboard displays information on the rate of recorded family incidents, the demographics of the members involved, rate of incidents with children involved and number of cases of intervention.

The data contained in this dashboard was extracted from the Victoria Police Law Enforcement Assistance Program on 18 July 2018.

Key Definitions

Family incident

An incident attended by Victoria Police where a Risk Assessment and Risk Management Report (also known as an L17 form) was completed.

Affected family member (AFM)

An 'affected family member' is the individual who is deemed to be affected by events occurring during the family incident. Where an affected family member has been affected by more than one other party within a family incident, they will be counted for each involvement.

Other party (0TH)

The other individual involved in a family incident is referred to as the 'other party'. The other party could be a current partner, former partner or a family member. Where the other party is involved with multiple affected family members, they will be counted for each involvement.

L17 form

An L 17 form refers to the Victoria Police Risk Assessment and Management Report that Victoria Police are required to complete after they have attended a family incident. The report is completed when family incidents, interfamilial-related sexual offences, and child abuse are reported to police.

Principal offences related to a family incident

Offences related to a family incident are recorded criminal offences which are linked to an incident where L 17 form was completed. Where there were multiple offences recorded within one incident, the principal offence refers to the assigned offence category which is the most serious offence.

Family Violence Intervention Order (FVIO)

Victoria Police can issue a Family Violence Safety Notice in order to immediately protect the affected family members before an intervention order is able to be heard in court.

Confidentialisation

In order to maintain confidentiality, person-based counts with a value of 3 or less are displayed as 's 3' and are given a value of 2 to calculate totals.

Rate per 100,000 population

The rate of affected family members per 100,000 population is calculated using the count of AFMs on original applications in a local government area and the estimated resident population (ERP) of that LGA. The rate is calculated using the following formula:
AFM rate = (Number of AFMs/ERP count) x 100,000

Relationship between the AFM and other party

The relationship between the AFM and other party is from the perspective of the AFM. For example, in an incident where the AFM is the mother of the 0TH, the relationship would be 'Family'.

Hazards/Risk Factors

The risk factors identified on the Risk Assessment and Risk Management Report are the evidence-based risk and vulnerability factors outlined in the Victoria Police Code of Practice for the Investigation of Family Violence. There are three types of hazards, those relating to the AFM, those relating to the other party and those present in the relationship.

Youth

Affected family members or other parties who have a recorded age of 17 years or younger.

Child present flag

A record which indicates whether one or more children were identified as having been present at the time of the incident.
